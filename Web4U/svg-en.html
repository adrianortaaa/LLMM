<path>

The most versatile element, used to draw complex shapes and curves via commands (move, line, curve, etc.).

<svg height="210" width="400" xmlns="">
  <path d="M150 0 L75 200 L225 200 Z" fill="orange" stroke="black" stroke-width="3" /> 
</svg>

<circle>

Circular element with center coordinates and radius.

<svg width="200" height="120" xmlns="https://www.w3schools.com/graphics/svg_circle.asp">
  <circle cx="60" cy="60" r="30" fill="#e44" />
</svg>

Back to Top ↑

<image>

Used to embed an image inside an SVG.

<svg height="200" width="300" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg"> 
  <image height="200" width="300" href="pulpitrock.jpg">
</svg>

<text>

Text inside an SVG document.

<svg height="30" width="200" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg">
  <text x="5" y="15" fill="red">I love SVG!</text>
</svg>

<rect>

The <rect> element is used to draw rectangles, defining its position, width, height, and optionally rounded corners.

<svg height="200" width="400" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg">
  <rect x="50" y="40" width="300" height="120" fill="lightgreen" stroke="black" stroke-width="3" rx="15" ry="15"/>
</svg>
